Today, we will learn how to create **fluorescent hydrogel spheres**. We will explain all the steps to correctly carry out this simple experiment.

## Required Materials

For this experiment, we will need the following materials:

1. **Hydrogel spheres**. A few of these will suffice, as they will significantly increase in size when hydrated.

![Unhydrated hydrogel spheres.](/images/contenido/creando-esferas-de-hidrogel-fluorescentes/esferas-de-hidrogel.webp)

2. **Fluorescein**. A small amount will also suffice.

![Container with fluorescein inside.](/images/contenido/creando-esferas-de-hidrogel-fluorescentes/fluoresceina.webp)

3. **Water**.
4. A **container** for preparing the solution and hydrating the spheres.

## Procedure

### Prepare the Solution

The first step is to prepare the solution in which we will hydrate the hydrogel spheres to make them fluorescent. This solution should contain **water and fluorescein**. The level of fluorescence of our spheres will depend on the amount of fluorescein used.

![Solution of water and fluorescein.](/images/contenido/creando-esferas-de-hidrogel-fluorescentes/disolucion.webp)

It's important to note that a small amount of fluorescein produces a strong color in the solution. Therefore, it's best to add this substance **gradually**.

### Immerse the Spheres in the Solution

Once our solution is ready and has a fluorescent color, we should **immerse the spheres** in the solution in a container.

Keep in mind that the spheres will significantly increase in size when hydrated, so it's crucial not to add too many at once, as they won't fit in the container once fully hydrated.

After a few minutes, you will observe the spheres beginning to swell as they hydrate, and after **several hours, they will reach their maximum size**.

## Illuminate with Ultraviolet Light

Once the hydrogel spheres are hydrated with fluorescein, you can illuminate them with UV light. You can use a UV flashlight (or a similar device) or a handy DIY method.

For this, you will need your **mobile phone** (or any flashlight), **tape**, and a **blue permanent marker**. Cover your phone's flashlight with a piece of tape, and color it with the blue permanent marker.

Repeat this process several times to create ultraviolet light.

You can take a stunning photograph of these spheres by darkening the working area and illuminating them with your phone's light. Simple and spectacular, isn't it?
